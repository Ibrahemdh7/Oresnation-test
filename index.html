UPS Journey Through Time

From a small messenger company in Seattle to the world's largest package delivery company

Over 115 Years of Innovation

A success story that began with $100 and a big dream, to become today a logistics empire connecting 220 countries and territories around the world

A journey from bicycle deliveries to a fleet of over 500 aircraft and 120,000 delivery vehicles

1907 - Humble Beginnings

In a basement below a saloon in Seattle, James Casey (19 years old) and Claude Ryan started the American Messenger Company

The service relied on foot and bicycle deliveries. Early customers would request delivery of messages, small packages, and even food from local restaurants

The company motto was "Best Service and Lowest Rates" - a principle that still stands today

1913 - The Motor Revolution

The company purchases its first delivery vehicle - a converted Model T Ford - marking a turning point in the company's history

With increasing demand for services, bicycles became insufficient. The first car allowed delivery of larger packages and longer distances

By the end of the year, the fleet expanded to 4 cars, and the company began hiring professional drivers

1919 - Birth of United Parcel Service

The company expanded to Oakland, California - the first expansion outside Seattle. With this expansion came the new name: United Parcel Service

The distinctive brown color "Pullman Brown" was chosen for trucks - the same color as luxury train cars of that time

The reason? Brown hides dirt and gives an impression of professionalism and trust. This color became part of UPS identity to this day

1929 - Pioneering Air Transportation

UPS becomes the first delivery company to offer air transportation for packages! The service began between major cities on the West Coast

The company used private aircraft to transport packages between Seattle, San Francisco, and Los Angeles - a journey that took days by truck became just a few hours

This bold move put UPS at the forefront and distinguished it from competitors for decades to come

1953 - Blue Label Air

Launch of "Blue Label Air" service - a revolutionary two-day air delivery service at reasonable prices

For the first time, small businesses and individuals could send packages by air without paying exorbitant prices

The service was available between major cities in the United States, forming a model for modern express delivery services

1975 - Complete Coverage and International Expansion

Historic achievement! UPS becomes the first private delivery company to serve every address in the 48 contiguous United States

In the same year, the company started "Golden Link" service - the first regular international service to Canada

Shortly after, service expanded to West Germany, marking the beginning of UPS's European presence

1985 - Next Day Air

Launch of Next Day Air service - next-day delivery 100% guaranteed or money back!

This service changed the rules of the game in the shipping industry. Companies could now rely on guaranteed delivery for sensitive documents and packages

The service included full tracking and proof of delivery - technologies that were very advanced at that time

1988 - UPS Airlines

Birth of UPS Airlines! The company obtains a private airline license and becomes an independent airline

It started with 14 Boeing 727 aircraft, but the fleet grew rapidly to become one of the largest air cargo fleets in the world

Louisville Airport in Kentucky became the main hub "Worldport" - one of the most advanced shipping centers in the world

1999 - Historic Public Offering

After 92 years as a private company, UPS goes public in the largest IPO in American history at that time

The company raised $5.47 billion in one day! The stock price opened at $50 and closed at $68

This move enabled UPS to expand rapidly, acquire new companies, and enter new markets around the world
